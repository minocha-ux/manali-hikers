Our Story

Trail knowledge passed down, not looked up.

Manoj Kumar's family are Khampa — Buddhist nomads of the north-eastern Himalaya who moved with the seasons and their herds, and who traced many of the passes we still cross today, long before they had names on any trekking map.

His father settled at Shaminala, near Jagatsukh in the Kullu valley, and began guiding the earliest parties over these routes. Manoj turned that inheritance into Manali Hikers in 1998.

"We don't read these mountains from a map. We were raised on them."

Today he holds Advanced and Method-of-Instruction qualifications from the ABVIMAS Mountaineering Institute in Manali, and has personally summited ten peaks between 6,000 and 6,500 metres. Every guide on the team trains to that same standard.
