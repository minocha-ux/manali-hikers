Malana Trek

⏱ 10 days ▲ 3,660m Moderate May to November
Overview

One of the most enchanting treks in the Kullu valley, combining deep forest, wide hanging valleys and the bleak, moraine-strewn landscape around the Malana glacier. The route ends at Malana, a tribal village considered one of the world's oldest democracies, where visitors need the village head's permission before entering certain sacred sites.

Day by day

Day 1 216m

Arrival in Delhi

Reception at the airport, transfer to hotel.

Day 2 2,000m

Delhi to Manali

Overnight tourist bus, 570km.

Day 3 2,000m

Manali

Old Manali, Hadimba temple, Shiva temple and town monastery.

Day 4 2,135m

Manali to Rumsu

Drive to Nagar, explore the castle and Roerich gallery, then climb to the traditional village of Rumsu.

Day 5 2,378m

Rumsu to Chiklani

A steady climb to base camp through oak, poplar, pine and rhododendron.

Day 6 3,660m

Chiklani to Nagruni via Chandrakhani Pass

A short climb to the pass gives extensive views of the snow-capped ranges and the Kullu valley, then an easy forest walk to camp.

Day 7 2,652m

Nagruni to Malana

Descend to Malana — a village of distinct dialect and customs, considered one of the world's oldest democracies.

Day 8 2,000m

Malana to Manali via Manikaran

Descend to the road head and visit the Manikaran hot-spring pilgrimage site.

Day 9 216m

Manali to Delhi

Overnight bus.

Day 10 216m

Arrive Delhi

Half-day sightseeing, then onward flight.
