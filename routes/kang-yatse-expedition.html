Kang Yatse Expedition

⏱ 14 days ▲ 6,200m Technical 1 June to 30 October Minimum 7 persons per group
Overview

Based in Leh-Ladakh, this climb offers the chance to reach 6,200m without technical climbing skills, or 6,400m with basic technical ability. Challenging yet interesting on its snowy, moderately technical upper slopes, Kang Yatse is often chosen as a climber's first Himalayan summit.

Day by day

Day 1 3,400m

Leh to Zinchen

Transfer to Spituk to meet the team, then hike west along the Indus into Hemis National Park to Zinchen.

Day 2 4,500m

Zinchen to base of Ganda La

Down the gorge, multiple river crossings, ascend toward the foot of Ganda La.

Day 3 4,800m

Yurutse to Skiu via Ganda La

Cross Ganda La then descend into the Markha valley to camp at Skiu.

Day 4 3,710m

Skiu to Markha

Several river crossings along the Markha valley to the village of Markha.

Day 5 3,750m

Markha to Tchatchutse

Past the monastery of Humlung near Hankar, up a narrower valley to Tchatchutse.

Day 6 5,040m

Tchatchutse to base of Kang Yatse

Past yak pastures to base camp, with an evening acclimatisation walk to 5,400m.

Day 7 6,200m

Summit day — Kang Yatse to Nimaling

Start around 2am; 5–7 hours to the summit on snowy, moderately technical slopes, then descend to Nimaling.

Day 8 4,878m

Security day at Nimaling

Weather buffer day; walk among the high-altitude shepherd pastures if conditions allow.

Day 9 5,150m

Nimaling to Shang via Kongmaru La

Cross the magnificent river bed and a difficult slope to Shang.

Day 10 3,500m

Shang to Leh

Drive back to Leh.
