Manali–Leh Ladakh Motorbike Tour

⏱ 10 days ▲ 5,359m Moderate June to October Minimum 6 people
Overview

A motorbike tour from Manali, India's outdoor sports capital, over the Tanglang La (5,359m) and the world's highest motorable pass, Khardung La (5,540m), to the edge of Tibet. Highlights include the ancient Alchi Monastery, the markets of Leh, a camel safari at Hundar in the Nubra Valley and a soak in the Panamik hot springs.

Day by day

Day 1 2,000m

Manali

Airport welcome, meet the team, local sightseeing.

Day 2 3,350m

Manali to Keylong via Rohtang

115km over Rohtang Jot to become familiar with the bike and the roads.

Day 3 4,000m

Keylong to Sarchu via Baralacha La

Over the Baralacha La; a night camping under the stars.

Day 4 5,359m

Sarchu to Leh

Over Nakee La, Lachulung La and Tanglang La — a long high-altitude ride.

Day 5 3,500m

Leh — sightseeing & Alchi

Arrange the Nubra permit; ride to the oldest monastery in Ladakh, Alchi.

Day 6 5,540m

Leh to Nubra via Khardung La

Over the world's highest motorable pass into Nubra; camp two nights.

Day 7 3,200m

Nubra Valley

Camel safari at Hunder and the Panamik hot springs.

Day 8 3,500m

Nubra to Leh

Return over Khardung La.

Day 9 4,300m

Leh to Pangong Lake via Chang La

To the vast, part-Tibetan Pangong Lake; camp on its banks.

Day 10 3,500m

Pangong to Leh

Return to Leh; final markets and shopping.
