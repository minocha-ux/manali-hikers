Bara Bhangal Shepherds’ Trail

⏱ 12 days ▲ 4,878m Challenging July to October Minimum 2, maximum 14 persons Kalihani and Thamsar Passes Heritage route
Overview

Also known as the Shepherds' Trail, this route is reserved for those experienced in rugged backcountry travel and confident with hiking and camping logistics. One of the more demanding treks on offer, it crosses from the lush Kullu valley to the stark landscapes of the Bara Bhangal range — a rewarding route for travellers seeking isolation and immersion in nature.

Day by day

Day 1 2,050m

Arrival in Manali

Participants arrive in Manali.

Day 2 3,017m

Manali to Lama Dugh

Steep climb through thick forest with no water point after Manali; views of Indrasan, Deo Tibba and the Bara Shigri glacier peaks open up. Camp on an alpine plateau thick with flowers.

Day 3 3,400m

Lama Dugh to Riyali Thach

A steep ridge below Khand Pari Tibba (4,000m), with views over Manali and the upper Kullu valley.

Day 4 4,010m

Riyali Thach to Base of Kalihani Pass

Open meadows, a steep descent to a stream crossing, then a steep climb to camp at the base of Kalihani Pass.

Day 5 4,725m

Base of Kalihani to Devi ki Marhi via Kalihani Pass

A long, arduous day crossing scree, moraine and the Kalihani glacier — 360° views of hanging glaciers, the Pir Panjal and Dhauladhar ranges — then descend past four glacial lakes.

Day 6 3,900m

Devi ki Marhi to Dal Marhi

Cross the Kalihani Nullah into oak and birch forest, mostly flat with a steep climb near the end.

Day 7 2,882m

Dal Marhi to Bara Bhangal

Down the Ravi river into deodar forest to the isolated village of Bara Bhangal.

Day 8 2,882m

Rest day at Bara Bhangal

Explore the village, home to the Gaddi tribe, seldom visited by outsiders.

Day 9 3,830m

Bara Bhangal to Mardh

Climb through pine, deodar and birch forest toward the Thamsar glacier, base of the next big pass.

Day 10 4,878m

Mardh to Plachak via Thamsar Pass

Steep ascent over moraine, scree and glacier to Thamsar Pass — the trek's highest point — with first views of the lush Kangra valley, then a long descent to Plachak.

Day 11 2,440m

Plachak to Rajgundha

The easiest day of the trek: flat pine forest walking with views over the open Kangra valley.

Day 12 2,310m

Rajgundha to Billing

Easy walk through pine forest to Billing, end of the trek — vehicle transfer to Bir or Dharamshala.
