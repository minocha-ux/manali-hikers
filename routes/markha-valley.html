Markha Valley

⏱ 8 days ▲ 5,200m Moderate June to October Kongmaru La (5,200m), Ganda La (4,900m)
Overview

One of the most popular treks in Ladakh, giving a real feel for the region's stark landscape. Its appeal lies in the diversity of terrain — deep gorges and huge canyon walls giving way to high alpine meadows where nomadic shepherds graze goats, sheep and yaks. There are multiple entry points into the valley; this itinerary follows the most popular route.

Day by day

Day 1 3,500m

Arrival in Leh

Participants arrive and rest to acclimatise.

Day 2 3,380m

Leh to Spituk to Zingchen

Drive to Spituk then a long barren stretch into the lush Zingchen valley where the Rumbak river meets the Indus.

Day 3 3,900m

Zingchen to Base of Ganda La

Enter Hemis National Park — look out for snow leopard and blue sheep — climb past a couple of villages.

Day 4 4,900m

Base of Ganda La to Skiu via Ganda La

Ascend to Ganda La, then a gradual descent and easy walk to camp at Skiu.

Day 5 3,300m

Skiu to Markha

Easy walk along the Markha river, crossing it several times, to the valley's largest village.

Day 6 3,800m

Markha to Thachungtse

Past old stone mills, mani walls, a castle and a monastery, then out toward Thachungtse.

Day 7 4,878m

Thachungtse to Nimaling

The easiest day — a gradual ascent over the open meadows of Nimaling.

Day 8 5,200m

Nimaling to Shang Sumdo via Kongmaru La

Cross the trek's highest pass, then a steep descent leveling out into the Shang gorge to Shang Sumdo, where a vehicle meets you for Leh.
