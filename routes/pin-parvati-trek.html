Pin Parvati Trek

⏱ 13 days ▲ 5,300m Challenging July to end of September Minimum 2, maximum 14 persons Pin Parvati Pass, Kunzam La and Rohtang Pass
Overview

One of the region's most spectacular treks, crossing from the lush Parvati valley in Kullu to the stark, barren Pin valley in Spiti via the high-altitude Mantalai Lake and the Pin Parvati Pass. The route skirts the Pin Valley National Park, known for elusive snow leopards, and the Great Himalayan National Park, home to rare birds such as the Western Tragopan.

Day by day

Day 1 1,760m

Arrive Bhuntar to Manikaran

Vehicle transfer to Manikaran.

Day 2 3,020m

Manikaran to Barsheni to Khirganga

A hot spring where Lord Shiva is said to have meditated.

Day 3 3,285m

Khirganga to Tunda Bhuj

Dense forest and flower-strewn meadows, a steep gradual climb.

Day 4 3,620m

Tunda Bhuj to Thakur Kuan

Waterfalls, lakes and meadows.

Day 5 3,800m

Thakur Kuan to Odi Thach

Cross the Pandu Bridge, a single-rock span said to be built by the Pandavas in exile.

Day 6 4,115m

Odi Thach to Mantalai Lake

Gradual climb to the source lake of the Parvati river, beneath the Mantalai Glacier.

Day 7 4,300m

Mantalai to base of Pin Parvati Pass

Ascend to the base camp of Mount Parvati, also the base of the pass.

Day 8 5,300m

Base of Pin Pass over the pass

A steep climb over Pin Parvati Pass — views of the Hamta region on one side, the Pin valley of Spiti on the other.

Day 9 3,700m

Campsite over Pin Pass to Tiai

Along the Pin river into Pin Valley National Park — snow leopard, musk deer and ibex country.

Day 10 3,650m

Tiai to Mud

Through the stark landscape of Spiti to the village of Mud.

Day 11 3,650m

Mud to Gulling to Kaza

Rough-road walk with views of Spiti's "middle country", then jeep transfer to Kaza.

Day 12 3,650m

Monastic sightseeing around Kaza

Ki monastery, Kibber village, Dhankar and Tabo monasteries.

Day 13 2,050m

Kaza to Manali

Drive via Kunzum Pass, Keylong, Koksar and Rohtang Pass back to Manali.
