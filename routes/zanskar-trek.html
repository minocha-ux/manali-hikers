Zanskar Trek

⏱ 10 days ▲ 5,060m Challenging Monsoon: July to September
Overview

For those seeking adventure and untouched natural beauty, this is one of the tougher routes in Ladakh, running from Padum to Lamayuru through barren, remote terrain and over the high Sengge La. It is recommended only for trekkers who are already well acclimatised to altitude before setting out.

Day by day

Day 1 3,600m

Padum to Karsha / Rinum

Visit Karsha, the largest and most important monastery in Zanskar, built in the 15th century.

Day 2 3,400m

Karsha to Pishu

Easy trail mostly following the Zanskar River, with a detour to the longest hanging bridge over it.

Day 3 3,550m

Pishu to Hanumil

Left-bank trail, a moraine or two to cross.

Day 4 4,200m

Hanumil to Snertse

Away from the river, up to the plateau and the foot of Parfi La.

Day 5 3,800m

Snertse to Lingshed

Ascent to Hanuma La pass through gorges past a sheep fold.

Day 6 5,060m

Lingshed to base of Sengge La

Via Margum La and Kuba La to the base of Sengge La.

Day 7 3,750m

Sengge La to Photoksar

Climb to Boumitse La then descend to Photoksar.

Day 8 3,500m

Photoksar to Hanupatta

A long ascent to Sirsir La.

Day 9 3,300m

Hanupatta to Wanla

Continue across the slope into the gorge.

Day 10 3,510m

Wanla to Lamayuru

Along the Shill Kong, through Shilla village and a dry gorge to Lamayuru.
