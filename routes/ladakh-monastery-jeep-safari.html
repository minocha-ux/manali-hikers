Ladakh Monastery Jeep Safari

⏱ 15 days ▲ 3,850m Easy
Overview

Leh Ladakh's monasteries are each known for their own festival, celebrated to mark the founding of the gompa, the birthday of a patron saint, or a major event in Tibetan Buddhist history — with feasting, singing and dancing, mostly in winter but some in summer. This safari visits the region's monasteries from Leh through Zanskar to Padum and back.

Day by day

Day 1 216m

Arrive Delhi

Transfer to hotel.

Day 2 3,500m

Delhi to Leh

Flight to Leh, rest and acclimatise.

Day 3 3,500m

Leh — Shey, Thiksey, Hemis

Three of Ladakh's most important monasteries.

Day 4 3,100m

Leh to Alchi

Basgo Palace and the 18th-century Likir monastery en route.

Day 5 3,510m

Alchi to Lamayuru

The dramatic "moonland" monastery on its high promontory.

Day 6 2,700m

Lamayuru to Kargil

Morning religious ceremony at Lamayuru, then drive to Kargil.

Day 7 3,900m

Kargil to Rangdum

Up the Suru valley past the twin peaks of Nun and Kun.

Day 8 3,600m

Rangdum to Padum

Rangdum monastery, then on to the capital of Zanskar.

Day 9 3,600m

Padum

Karsha, Sani, Thongde, Zangla and Bardan monasteries.

Day 10 3,600m

Padum

Continued exploration of Zanskar's villages and gompas.

Day 11 3,900m

Padum to Rangdum

Camp at Rangdum.

Day 12 2,700m

Rangdum to Kargil

Return to Kargil; explore the Turkish-style old town.

Day 13 3,500m

Kargil to Leh

Basgo monastery en route.

Day 14 3,500m

Leh

Leisure or further monasteries.

Day 15 216m

Leh to Delhi

Flight to Delhi.
