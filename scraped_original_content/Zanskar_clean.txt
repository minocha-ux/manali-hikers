Zanskar Trek

The Zanskar trek is for those who seek adventure and to explore the barren, untouched natural beauty, this trek is a great experience. One of the tougher routes in Ladakh, it is recommended for those well acclimatized.

Trip Detail

Region: Ladakh

Max Altitude: 5060 mtr

Season: Monsoon Jul - Sep

Duration: 10 Days

Trip Begins : – Padum (District HQ of Zanskar valley)

Trip Ends : Lamayuru near leh

ITINERARY:

Day 01: Padam – Karsha / Rinum

Duration:  5 hours

Karsha is the largest and the most important monastery in the Zanskar region. The monastery was built in the 15th Century and contains beautiful statues of Buddha, Shakyamuni, Tsongkhapa and Avolokeshwara. It also contains a big library.

Day 02: Karsha / Rinum – Pishu

Duration:  6 hours

The trail is easy, often following the Zanskar River. Before Pishu, make a little detour to see the longest hanging bridge over the Zanskar.

Day 03: Pishu – Hanumil

Duration:  5 hours

The trail continues on the left bank. While the path is mostly easy, a moraine or two needs to be crossed on the way.

Day 04: Hanumil – Snertse

Duration:  6 hours

The path now leads away from the Zanskar River, up to the plateau, then to the foot of the Parfi La.

Day 05: Snertse – Lingshed

Duration:  7 hours

The ascent to the Hanuma La pass goes through gorges and then passes a sheep fold.

Day 06: Lingshed – Base of Sengge La (5060m)

Duration:  6 hours
It’s an hour’s easy climb up to Margum La (4100m). Descending from there, you reach the Zanskar River again. After passing the villages of Gongma and Skiumpata, a steep ascent will take you to Kuba La (3800m). Continue climbing up to the base of Sengge la (5060m).

Day 07: Sengge La – Photoksar (3750m)

Duration:  7 hours

After an easy walk, climb up to Boumitse La (4200m). Then descend to Photoksar.

Day 08: Photoksar – Hanupatta

Duration:  6 hours

A long ascent from Photoksar takes you to Sirsir La.

Day 09: Hanupatta – Wanla

Duration:  5 hours

From Hanupatta, continue across the slope until entering the gorge.

Day 10: Wanla – Lamayuru

Duration:  5 hours

Go up the Shill Kong along the right bank, cross it then arrive in Shilla village. After passing this village you will arrive at a broad chorten, then follow a dry gorge as far as the Prinkiti La (3762m).

Customer Itinerary

Reaching Leh :

By Air: Leh is well connected to Delhi and a number of operators fly this sector. This is the easiest and best option.

By Road : You can travel to Leh from Manali or Srinagar. Many buses and private cabs operate on both the sectors.
