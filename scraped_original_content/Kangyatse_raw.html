Kangyatse Expedition

This climb offers you an opportunity to reach a height of 6200m without technical skills, and 6400 m with basic technical skills. The climb on Kangyatse is challenging and interesting, ideal for anyone’s first Himalayan summit.

Trip Detail
Region: Leh-Ladakh
Max Altitude:5150 mtr.
Season:01June   to 30 October
Duration:13Nights/14Days
Age Group:Between 15 to 60 years.
Minimum Numbers per Group:7
Cost:On Request

ITINERARY:

Day 01): Leh-Zinchen(3400m), 5hrs
transfer up to Spituk where you will meet the team pf the Expedition. The hike begins following the banks of Indus westward. We are going to cross a wide tray in the shade of the chaine of stoke, then enter by a throat inside the National park of Hemis. Late in the afternoon, arrived at Zinchen .

Day 02): Zinchen-Base of Ganda La (4500m), 5hrs
 Today we go down the throatand cross the river of numerous times. A landscape of multicolored rocks and poplars through a vast tray appears then. Continuation towards Yurutse. Possibility of going to meet a local family. Continuously ascend to the foot of the  Gandla Pass.

Day 03): Yurutse-Skiu (3400m) via Ganda La (4800m) 6hrs
Of Yurutse , we perceive beautiful landscape along the mountain. The crossing of a small valley is followed by an ascent zigzag up to the tray. We continue then about 3hrs along the mountain up to the pass Ganda La. A long easy descend towards Shingo leads us through parades of magnificent landscapes. Having come down approximately 2hrs, we arrive in the valley of Markha, where a beautiful ground serve us as campsite to Skiu. Overnight Camp.

Day 04): Skiu-Markha , (3710m), 7hrs
Today we will walk on the right bank of Markha Valley, attractive path with a lot of vegetations. Several successive crossing left bank , right bank lead us  to  Chaulak (Few Houses). New  crossing which are linked before achieving, after a short rise , but stiff, the beautiful village  of Markha. We establish the camp near the river. Overnight Camp.
Day 05): Markha-Tchatchutse, (3750m), 5hrs
The trek goes back up the main valley , the right bank. The path is sometimes difficult in the bed of the river. Before arriving  at the village of Hankar, we visit the monastery of Humlung, along the river. Little beyond the Hankar, we abandon a wide valley on our right. We take a narrow valley going up higher and which obliges us to cross a bridge to has the left bank to reach the pasture of Tchatchutse . Overnight Camp.

Day 06): Tchatchutse-Base of Kangyatse (5040m), 5hrs
The path rises very gradually , the river being rather far in our left. We continue near a small lake , then cross the pastures where are often herds of the Yaks. It is one of the most beautiful high valley of Ladakh. Camp at feel of the summit . In the evening , stroll of gratitude for the climbers until 5400m to acclimatize to the height. Overnight Camp.

Day 07): Kangyatse Base-Summit-Nimaling
Today we will ascend around 02:00am and with 05to 07hrs of ascent are approximately needed to reach the summit , easily accessible by a snowy but little bit technical slopes . After this summit return in the camp of Nimaling. Those who are not interested in the ascent will have been able to walk on the beautiful tray of Nimaling , in the discover of the shepherds  of height. Overnight Camp.
Day 08): Security Day
This day is necessary in case of bed weather the day before for the ascent of Kang Yatse , If we might weather in the day time , we leave then for a new stroll on the meeting of the shepherds and there herbs. Return in the afternoon and rest . Overnight Camp.
Day 09):Nimaling-Shang via Konmaru la Pass (5150m), 7-8hrs
. The track cross the magnificent bed of the river and continues by a difficult slope for the horses. After several crossings of the river , arrive at Shang. Beautiful camp in copses. Overnight Camp.
Day 10): Shang-Leh-60km, 2hrs

After  breakfast drive  to Leh.
